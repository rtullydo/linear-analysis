Suppose that \(f \in C^1[0,1]\text{.}\) Then for \(x \in [0,1]\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} f(x) = a_1 J_\alpha(\la_{\alpha,1}x) + a_2 J_\alpha(\la_{\alpha,2}x) + \ldots = \sum_{n = 1}^\infty a_n J_\alpha (\la_{\alpha, n} x), \end{equation*}

where the coefficients \(a_i\) are given by the projection formula

\begin{equation*} a_i = \frac{\ip{f}{J_{\alpha}(\la_{\alpha,i}x)}}{\norm{J_{\alpha}(\la_{\alpha,i}x)}^2} = \frac{2}{\left[ J_{\alpha + 1}(\la_{\alpha,i})\right]^2} \int_0^1 x f(x) J_\alpha(\la_{\alpha, i}x)\, dx. \end{equation*}
