Theorem 2.1.3.

For a series of the form given in (2.1.1), exactly one of the following holds.

  1. \(r = 0:\) \(\ps\) converges only for \(x = 0\text{.}\)
  2. \(r = \infty:\) \(\ps\) converges for every \(x \in \R\text{.}\)
  3. \(r\) is finite: There is a constant \(r > 0\) so that \(\ps\) converges for \(x \lt \abs{r}\) and diverges for \(x > \abs{r}\text{.}\)
